What We Are Building — Read This First

Before you dial a single number, you must internalise what Cuemath actually is. Every call, every message, every follow-up is built on this.

The Core Truth

Cuemath is not a tutoring platform. We are not selling a subscription. We are inviting a parent to start their child's MathFit journey — and helping them understand why stopping is never the right decision.

MathFit — What It Means

MathFit is a child's mathematical fitness: the ability to reason clearly, spot patterns, explain ideas in their own words, and solve unfamiliar problems with confidence. It is built over time through consistent practice with expert coaching. It compounds.

The Mental Gym Analogy — Use This On Every Call

"Math is not a subject to be studied. It's a gym for the mind. You don't become physically fit by watching exercise videos. You go, you practice, you get coached. Mathematical fitness works exactly the same way. Cuemath is that gym."

The Lead Funnel — End to End

This is the full journey a lead takes from slot received to enrollment. Every AC must know every stage and every action required at each stage.

The Core Objective of This Funnel

Filter aggressively at the top. Protect quality at the bottom. A lead who reaches the trial without understanding MathFit, without knowing the price, and without a laptop is a waste of everyone's time — yours, the parent's, and the teacher's. The AC's job is to qualify or disqualify every lead in the first conversation.

Which Leads Do ACs Work?

ACs only work leads where the parent has given a slot — i.e., leads in Slots Received status in Superleap. If a lead has not given a slot, the AC does not touch it. Open leads without a slot are not in the AC's queue.

Stage-by-Stage Action Guide

1
Slot Received — First Contact
CRM: Slots Received

What happened: A parent filled a lead form and selected a trial slot. This is when the lead enters the AC's queue. The parent has shown intent — they want a trial — but they have not yet spoken to anyone from Cuemath.

Your job: Connect with this lead within 48 hours of the slot being received. Deliver the full QL→TS pitch. This call is the most critical step in the entire funnel. If you don't connect here, the lead is essentially lost.

  • Apply the Slots Received filter in Superleap at 1:30 PM — this shows all leads that received a slot today or yesterday and have not yet been connected with
  • Call every lead in this list between 1:30 PM and 4:30 PM
  • Before dialing: note the child's name, grade, and channel source (to identify parent archetype)
  • Every connected call is a full QL→TS pitch — MathFit, laptop check, pricing, pro-rata refund. ~12–15 minutes.
📞 AC calls: Full QL→TS pitch (see Training Manual). ~12–15 min call.
💬 AC sends immediately after call: Stage 1 WhatsApp (AC Message Generator) — MathFit + pricing + curriculum + board alignment
1b
RNR — Lead Did Not Pick Up
CRM: Slots Received

What happened: AC called but the customer did not pick up. The 48-hour connect window is still open. Keep trying.

Retry logic — must connect within 48 hours of slot being received:

  • Attempt 1 (Day 0): Dialer, during 1:30–4:30 PM calling block
  • Attempt 2 (Day 1): Dialer, next day calling block
  • Attempt 3 (Day 1, separate time): Personal number, with call recording ON
  • If still RNR after 3 attempts: Drop the non-responsive WhatsApp below. Do not call again until lead responds or until D+2 (see Stage 2).
Non-Responsive WhatsApp — Drop This After 3 Failed Attempts

"Hi [Parent Name]! This is [AC Name] from Cuemath. I've tried reaching you a couple of times — I completely understand you're busy! I wanted to share a quick note about what we're doing for [Child Name]'s math journey. Cuemath is not tutoring — we're building what we call MathFit: the ability to think clearly, reason independently, and solve unfamiliar problems with confidence. 1:1 with an expert teacher, fully aligned to [Board] curriculum. When you get a moment, just reply here and I will connect with you and I will share everything. — [AC Name], Cuemath 🚀"

2
D+2 — Trial is the Day After Tomorrow
CRM: Slots Received / Confirmation Pending

What happened: The trial is 2 days away. This is the second communication window. What happens now depends entirely on whether the AC connected with this parent before.

Sub-case A — AC Previously Connected (Not RNR)

The parent has already spoken to the AC, received the full pitch, and knows what Cuemath is. The Cuemath system has sent them a confirmation request. AC does not call — send a WhatsApp reminder to confirm attendance. Keep it warm and brief.

💬 AC sends: Brief WhatsApp — "Hi [Parent], just a reminder that your trial is the day after tomorrow. Please confirm your attendance so your teacher can be assigned. Looking forward to it! — [AC Name]"
Sub-case B — AC Never Connected (Still RNR)

This parent has never spoken to anyone from Cuemath. The trial is in 2 days and they have no context about what MathFit is, what the price is, or what to expect. The AC must call — and this call is the full QL→TS pitch. The goal is not just confirmation of attendance; it's making sure this parent fully understands Cuemath before they walk into the trial.

📞 AC calls: Full QL→TS pitch. ~12–15 min call. Get attendance confirmation + complete parent briefing.
💬 AC sends immediately after connected call: Stage 1 WhatsApp — MathFit + pricing + curriculum
Critical Rule

A teacher cannot be mapped until the parent confirms attendance. No confirmation = no teacher = no trial. Every unconfirmed D+2 lead must be actioned during the morning check (11:00–11:30 AM).

3
Teacher Mapped — Trial Confirmed for Tomorrow
CRM: Trial Scheduled / Login Sent

What happened: Parent confirmed attendance. Teacher has been mapped. The trial is locked in for tomorrow.

Your job: By EOD, send login credentials to every customer whose trial is scheduled for tomorrow. Also check whether any of tomorrow's trials have been cancelled — and if so, call those customers tonight.

  • At EOD, filter in Superleap: trials scheduled for tomorrow with status Confirmed / Teacher Mapped
  • For each lead: open the student's PII page in Superleap to get the username (password = same as username)
  • Use AC Message Generator Stage 4 to generate and send the Login Credentials WhatsApp to each
  • Also at EOD: check if any of tomorrow's confirmed trials have been cancelled by the system or tutor
  • If a cancellation is found: call the customer directly — do not send a re-slot WhatsApp. Speak to them.
  • If the customer confirms they still want the trial → fill the manual mapping request form to get a teacher assigned
💬 AC sends (EOD, for all trials tomorrow): Stage 4 WhatsApp — Login credentials (username, password, Leap link, device instructions)
📞 AC calls (if cancellation found): Connect with customer directly. If they confirm → fill manual mapping form.
4
Day of Trial
CRM: Trial Scheduled

What happened: Today is the trial day. The parent received login credentials last night. They have everything they need.

Your job: No reminders. No messages before the trial. At the exact trial start time, check whether the parent has joined. If they have — let the class run. If they haven't — call immediately.

  • At the exact start time of each trial today, check Superleap to confirm the parent has joined (e.g., trial at 6:00 PM → check at 6:00 PM)
  • Parent joined → no action. The class is running. You will call within 60 minutes of completion.
  • Parent not joined within 2–3 minutes → call immediately. Try to get them into the class.
  • If parent goes RNR on the spot call (not picking up despite multiple attempts) → the lead stage moves to Re-slot Pending. Send the Re-slot Pending WhatsApp message to the customer immediately on the spot.
📞 AC calls on the spot if parent hasn't joined within 2–3 minutes of trial start.
💬 AC sends (only if RNR on spot call): Re-slot Pending WhatsApp — on the spot, immediately.
4b
Re-slot Pending
CRM: Re-slot Pending

What is Re-slot Pending? A lead moves to Re-slot Pending when the trial did not happen and needs to be rescheduled. There are five triggers:

  • Parent failed to confirm attendance in time → slot auto-released
  • Parent cancelled their slot
  • Parent did not turn up for the trial (no-show) and went RNR on the AC's spot call
  • Tutor cancelled the class
  • System cancelled the slot
Note on Last 2 Situations

Tutor cancellation and system cancellation are already handled in Stage 3 (EOD check) and the morning check — the AC calls the customer directly in those cases. If the customer confirms, a manual mapping request is filled. If the trial still does not happen, the lead lands in Re-slot Pending and follows the protocol below.

Re-slot Pending weekly protocol — every Wednesday:

  • Every Wednesday, ACs devote 2–3 hours to working through all Re-slot Pending leads from the current month
  • For each lead: call the customer and attempt to re-slot. Share the relevant re-slot message on WhatsApp.
  • If the customer responds and picks a new slot → move the lead out of Re-slot Pending and back into the active funnel
  • If the customer does not respond on this Wednesday's attempt → mark the lead as Slots NI on the following Wednesday
📞 AC calls every Re-slot Pending lead during Wednesday's 2–3 hour block.
💬 AC sends: Re-slot WhatsApp to each lead called during Wednesday block. No response by next Wednesday → Slots NI.
5
Trial Done — Payment Follow-Up
CRM: Demo Done / Payment Pending

What happened: The trial class is complete. The parent and child have experienced Cuemath. This is the highest-leverage moment in the entire funnel. Emotion peaks right now — and fades fast.

Your job: Call within 60 minutes of the trial getting done. Every minute beyond that is conversion probability lost. On an approved off day, the rule applies from the next active working day — on any active day, 60 minutes is non-negotiable.

  • Call within 60 minutes of trial completion — check Superleap for the trial end time
  • Before calling: check teacher's trial notes and Talk Meter score in Superleap
  • After the call: send Stage 6 WhatsApp immediately — whether they said yes, thinking, or no
  • Send payment link within 15 minutes of a YES
  • For "thinking about it": get a specific follow-up date and time before ending the call — call at that exact time
📞 AC calls: Full TD→P conversion call (see Training Manual). Within 60 minutes of trial completion.
💬 AC sends immediately after call: Stage 6 WhatsApp — Trial insights + recommended plan + all pricing
6
Enrolled
CRM: Enrolled (auto-updated on payment)

What happened: Payment is done. As soon as the customer pays, the lead stage changes to Enrolled automatically in Superleap. The teacher also receives an automatic notification. The MathFit journey has officially begun.

Your job: As soon as you see the lead move to Enrolled, send the welcome message. Nothing else is needed — the system has handled the stage change and teacher notification.

  • Open AC Message Generator → Stage 7 (Welcome — Enrolled)
  • Enter Parent Name, Student Name, the plan they enrolled in, and your name — then generate and send
💬 AC sends: Stage 7 Welcome Message (AC Message Generator → Stage 7 — Welcome Enrolled)

The Daily Routine

This is the exact structure of every AC's day. Follow it without deviation. The order is not random — it is designed to protect teacher bandwidth, maximise conversions, and ensure no lead goes uncalled within 48 hours.

Why This Structure Exists

The problem with the previous flow: ACs were spending 2 to 2.5 hours every day calling D+2 leads to chase confirmation — calling parents who had already received a system confirmation request, asking them to do something they were going to do on their own. This left the highest-value work — demo follow-ups and referral leads — to the afternoon when energy is lower and parents are less available.

This also meant unqualified leads were reaching the trial — parents without laptops, without paying capacity, outside K–8. That wastes teacher time and AC time downstream.

If we are a premium brand, we must present ourselves as one from the first contact — not after the trial. As soon as a lead comes in, the parent should know exactly what Cuemath is, what to expect from the trial, and what the investment looks like. The AC should know the parent's requirements, expectations, and how they found Cuemath — so together we can give them the experience they came for, not a generic demo.
