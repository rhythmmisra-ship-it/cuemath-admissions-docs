Section 1

The QL→TS Call — Qualifying Pitch

Target duration: 12–15 minutes. This is not a booking call — it is a qualification and ideology call. Every parent who proceeds to a trial must understand MathFit, know the price, and confirm they have a laptop. If they fail any filter, do not push them to trial.

Before You Dial — Check All Four

1. Child's name and grade — K–8 only. Grade 9+ is not a lead for us.
2. Board — CBSE / ICSE / IB / Cambridge / State — know it before you call.
3. Channel source — referral leads are warmer; perf marketing leads need more qualification.
4. Form a hypothesis on parent archetype — Analytical / Concerned / Ambitious / Hesitant. You'll confirm it in the first 2 minutes.

1
Warm Open + Confirm Details — 1 min
Build trust immediately. Parents who feel known stay on the line.
AC Says

"Hi, am I speaking with [Parent Name]? Hi [Name], this is [AC Name] calling from Cuemath — the MathFit programme your child is registered with. How are you doing today?"

[Wait for response.]

"[Name], I have [Child's Name] here in my system, in Grade [X]. Is that right?" [Confirm.] "And they're following the [Board] curriculum?" [Confirm.]

"Perfect. Is this a good time to talk?"

AC Instruction

If they say "not a good time" — ask: "Of course — when would work better? Later today or tomorrow morning?" Lock a specific time. Do not hang up without a callback time.

If they agree — move immediately to discovery questions. Do not start pitching yet.

2
Discovery Questions — 3–4 min
Listen more than you speak. These answers shape your entire pitch.
AC Instruction

Ask all five questions. Do not rush. The parent's answers tell you their archetype, their fear, and their expectation. Reflect the answer back before asking the next question — this shows you're listening, not just collecting data.

AC Says — 5 Discovery Questions

Q1: "What made you reach out to Cuemath — is there something specific about [Child's Name]'s maths that you're looking to improve?" [Listen fully. Don't interrupt.]

Q2: "How did you come across Cuemath — was it through a friend, or did you see us online?" [Referral = warm. Online = needs more qualification.]

Q3: "How is [Child's Name] currently doing in maths at school — would you say they're confident, average, or finding it a bit challenging?" [This tells you FUAR dimension and GRIC gap.]

Q4: "Who is supporting [Child's Name] with maths at the moment — do they have a tutor or any extra help?" [Identifies competitor / switching situation.]

Q5: "Is [Child's Name] involved in any other extra-curricular activities — sports, music, coding?" [This helps with scheduling and commitment conversation.]

After Q1 — Identify Archetype

If they mention a specific problem (marks dropping, silly mistakes, weak concepts) → Concerned parent. Address the gap before the ideology.

If they mention data / board / curriculum → Analytical parent. Lead with FUAR framework and measurement.

If they mention IIT / Olympiad / "already doing well" → Ambitious parent. Position MathFit as the layer that takes strong to exceptional.

If they seem vague / say "just curious" → Hesitant/Busy. Keep it low friction. Let the trial do the talking.

3
The MathFit Reframe — 2–3 min
This is the ideology foundation. Every parent must understand this before the trial.
AC Says — AI Era Opening

"[Name], before I tell you about Cuemath, I want to share something that I think every parent today needs to hear."

"Our kids are growing up in a world being completely reshaped by artificial intelligence. The devices in our pockets can now crunch numbers faster and more accurately than any human ever could. So the question every parent should be asking is — what is the point of math as we knew it? What skills actually matter when calculation is basically free and everywhere?"

"The old way — speed tricks, memorisation, mechanical calculation — that's exactly what AI does. That's becoming obsolete for humans. The future belongs to something different: mathematical thinking. The ability to reason, to see patterns, to look at a problem you've never seen before — and not be afraid of it."

AC Says — Why MathFit Exists: The Story

"And I want to share where this idea came from — because it makes this real."

"Cuemath's founder — Manan Khurma — is an IIT Delhi alumnus. He built Cuemath into a global programme operating in 80+ countries. And from that vantage point, he had access to something most educators don't — he could observe how children from completely different countries and systems were learning math, and what was actually working."

"That observation led to a hypothesis: what if we designed a curriculum specifically around building mathematical thinking — not just speed and procedures? So Cuemath ran a pilot. Real teachers, real students, measured outcomes. The results were compelling."

"And then we did something very few edtech companies actually do: we spent six months training our teachers specifically in this method. Not general tutoring. The specific cue-based approach that builds MathFit. From 1st March this year, we've been live with the MathFit curriculum — grounded in research, tested in a pilot, and delivered by teachers who've gone through dedicated training for it."

AC Says — MathFit Definition

"That's what we mean when we say MathFit. It's not about who gets the answer fastest. It's about a child's ability to reason things out, see patterns, explain their ideas clearly, and tackle an unfamiliar problem with confidence — not fear."

"Think of it exactly like physical fitness. You don't become physically fit by watching exercise videos. You go, you practice, you get coached. Mathematical fitness works exactly the same way. Cuemath is that gym."

"So when you enrol [Child's Name] with Cuemath, you're not enrolling them in a math class. You're starting their MathFit journey."

For Concerned Parent — Insert This Before the Reframe

If parent mentioned a specific problem in Q1, address it first: "I hear you — the [specific issue they mentioned, e.g. silly mistakes / weak fractions] — and I want to tell you exactly how we address that. But first, let me share something about how we approach this at Cuemath..." Then go into the MathFit reframe. The parent must feel heard before they'll listen to ideology.

4
Make It Real — Grade-Specific Example — 1 min
Abstract ideology becomes concrete belief when the parent can picture it for their child's age.
AC Says — For Grade 3–5

"Let me make this real for you. In a Cuemath class for [Child's Name]'s age, we might give the child a scenario: 'You're opening an ice cream cart. What do you need to know to make a profit?' The child starts thinking about cost, selling price, footfall, weather. They're doing multiplication, division, percentages — but they don't even realise they're doing maths. They think they're running a business. That is MathFit in action."

AC Says — For Grade 6–8

"For [Child's Name]'s grade, we go deeper. We might introduce a real data set — say, cricket match scores or a stock price chart — and ask: 'What do you see? What's the pattern? What would you predict?' They're using algebra, statistics, logical reasoning — but the context is something that actually interests them. They start seeing math everywhere, not just in textbooks."

5
Cover Curriculum Objections Proactively — 30 sec
Say it before they ask it. Shows confidence and removes doubt.
AC Says

"Now I know what you're thinking — does this cover the school syllabus? Absolutely. And actually, the Cuemath curriculum has four parts — every one of them taught through the same cue-based, MathFit approach."

"First: Grade-wise and board-wise academic curriculum. CBSE, ICSE, IB, Cambridge — every concept, every chapter, fully aligned. But unlike school, [Child's Name] isn't being told the answer. They're being guided to discover it."

"Second: Mental Maths. Yes, this builds calculation speed. But the deeper benefit — and this is what parents notice after a few months — is focus. When a child consistently practices mental computation, they're training their brain to block out distraction. It builds concentration the same way meditation does. That carries into every subject."

"Third: Enrichment — real-world projects. Not textbook word problems. Live applied maths scenarios — what does a business need to break even? How does an architect use geometry? [Child's Name] connects math to the real world, which is exactly what builds the Application pillar of MathFit."

"Fourth: IMO and HOTS — High Order Thinking Skills. Olympiad-style problems. Pattern recognition, logical arguments, unfamiliar problem types. This is what separates strong school students from competitive exam performers."

"What changes is not what we teach. What changes is how we teach it — cue-based, MathFit, every single class."

6
1:1 Format + Teaching Method + Laptop Check — 1 min
Qualify the lead on laptop and confirm 1:1 understanding.
AC Says

"Cuemath is fully 1:1 — one teacher, one student, every single class. This MathFit ideology cannot work in a group. The teacher needs to respond to your child's thinking in real time — not 15 children at once."

"And the way we teach is what we call cue-based learning. If a child gives a wrong answer, the teacher doesn't correct immediately. They ask: 'Tell me why you think that.' The child thinks, discovers the error themselves, and corrects it. That discovery stays forever. A teacher who explains builds dependency. A teacher who cues builds confidence."

"One quick question — for the best trial experience, [Child's Name] will need a laptop or a desktop — not a phone, not a tablet. Do you have one at home?" [Confirm. If no laptop → this is a disqualifying signal. Note it.]

7
Draw Out the Parent's Expectation — 1 min
This is the most important question on the call. Stop talking after you ask it.
AC Says

"[Name] — as a parent, nobody knows [Child's Name] better than you. I've shared what we build at Cuemath. Now I want to understand: what is your expectation from us? What would you want to see in [Child's Name] — say six months from now — that would tell you that this has worked?"

[Stop. Wait. Listen fully. Do not fill the silence.]

AC Instruction — What to Do With the Answer

Whatever the parent says — repeat it back through the MathFit lens. Examples:

Parent says "I want him to stop making silly mistakes" → "Absolutely — that's a Fluency gap. Our programme is specifically designed to build accuracy through understanding, not repetition. Your child won't just practice more — they'll understand why the mistake is happening."

Parent says "I want her to love math" → "That's exactly GRIC — the mindset pillar of MathFit. Confidence and interest come when the child experiences success at the right level of difficulty. The teacher's job is to keep [child] in that sweet spot where the problem is just hard enough to be exciting."

Parent says "I want him ready for JEE / Olympiad" → "Perfect. The Reasoning pillar of MathFit is exactly what competitive exams test — unfamiliar problems, pattern recognition, logical arguments. We start building that from Grade 3 onwards."

8
Present Pricing — 1 min
State it with full confidence. No hedging. No apology. Then stop talking.
AC Says

"[Name], let me share how the programme is structured. Cuemath runs on 1:1 classes, twice a week. The plans are:"

"3 months — ₹30,680 including GST. 26 classes.
6 months — ₹55,224 including GST. 52 classes. About 10% better value.
12 months — ₹98,176 including GST. 104 classes. About 20% better value."

"My recommendation for [Child's Name] — based on everything you've told me — is the 12-month plan. MathFit takes time to compound. The first 3 months are foundation. Months 4–6 is where the thinking shift happens. Month 7 onwards is where parents say they can't believe how differently their child approaches problems. You need to be in for long enough for the compounding to show."

"And [Name] — the most important thing: we have a 100% pro-rata refund on unused classes. If at any point you feel it's not working, every class your child hasn't attended comes back to you. Zero risk. You only pay for what [Child's Name] uses."

[Stop. Let the parent respond. Do not offer a cheaper option before they ask.]

Objection

"It's too expensive" / "The fees are very high"

Counter

Angle 1 — Quality teaching is expensive. Poor quality teaching is more expensive. "Here's a story I think is worth sharing. A few years ago, an edtech company — not Cuemath — decided to scale fast by hiring 1,000 tutors at ₹15,000 a month. Revenue grew 500% in 12 months. Looked incredible. Then renewals came around. And they collapsed — because parents had paid for 'learning' and their children hadn't actually learned. The lesson: cheap teaching destroys trust faster than expensive teaching ever could. Quality teaching is expensive. Poor quality teaching ends up costing far more."

Angle 2 — Teacher quality: "When you pay ₹1,000 per class, your child's teacher earns ₹700–750 per session. That is what it takes to attract a genuinely qualified math educator who's been through six months of MathFit training. On platforms charging ₹300–500 per class, the teacher earns ₹150–200 per hour. No quality educator accepts that long-term. The tutor your child gets is a direct reflection of what you invest."

Angle 3 — Not the same product: "Whatever you're comparing us with — check first: is it 1:1 or a group class? Almost every cheaper option is a batch session. Cuemath is live 1:1 with a dedicated teacher, every single class. We are not in the same dimension."

Angle 4 — Renewal rate proves ROI: "Here's one number that tells the whole story: Cuemath's annual renewal rate is 75%+. That means if 100 families enrol today, more than 75 are back next year — voluntarily. The average across edtech in India is a fraction of that. Parents who get ROI renew. The renewal rate is the proof."

Angle 5 — Investment framing: "A child who builds strong mathematical foundations in Grades 3–8 develops logical thinking and academic confidence that carries into every subject and career. The returns on this investment far outlast the programme."

Objection

"Can you give a discount?" / "Any offer going on?"

Counter

"I want to be completely transparent — we have one price for every parent. No Diwali offers. No Republic Day discounts. No special rates for anyone. Here's why: imagine you enrolled today at a certain price, and three months later your neighbour — same teacher, same programme — enrolled during a 'sale' for less. Is that fair to you? In the education business, that kind of inconsistency destroys trust. Our price is fixed. No discounts, no exceptions. Every family deserves to be treated exactly the same."

Objection

"My friend / someone I know pays less at Cuemath"

Counter

"Thank you for bringing that up. In the past, there were instances where different pricing was offered to different families — and that was not the right approach. From February 2026, every family enrolling pays the same price. What your friend received was part of an older structure. What you see today is what every new family sees — no hidden deals, no negotiation."

Objection

"Why only 3, 6, 12 months? Can't we do monthly?"

Counter

"Three genuine reasons. First — teacher slot stability. If you book a teacher for one month, and next month another family books that teacher for a year, your child loses their teacher and has to start over. Our tenure structure guarantees your child keeps the same teacher throughout — that's the biggest predictor of MathFit progress. Second — personalization takes time. The teacher needs 4–6 weeks just to complete diagnostics and design a proper learning path. A monthly plan would end before the learning has even properly begun. Third — the compounding. MathFit is not a sprint. You won't see the full shift in 4 weeks."

The screen is the medium — the experience is 1:1 coaching."

Section 2

The TD→P Call — Version A

✓ Use This Version: Parent Was Briefed Before Trial (Full QL→TS Call Was Done)

The parent had a full conversation before the trial. They understand MathFit, they've heard the pricing, they know the ideology. Your job now is to connect what they experienced in the trial to the MathFit journey they already understand — and close.

Before You Call — Prepare in 2 Minutes

1. Check Superleap: teacher's trial notes. What did the teacher observe? What gaps or strengths came up?
2. Pull the parent archetype from your QL→TS notes. Use the same framing they connected with.
3. Recall the expectation they gave you on the intro call — this is your hook.
4. Decide your recommendation before you dial: 12-month or 6-month, 2/week or 3/week.

Timing Rule

Call within 60 minutes of the trial completing. Emotion is at its peak right now. Every hour of delay is conversion probability you're giving back. On an active working day — 60 minutes is non-negotiable.

1
Open With Trial Feedback — Not a Pitch — 1–2 min
The parent is still processing. Let them speak first. Their answer tells you everything.
AC Says

"Hi [Parent Name]! This is [AC Name] from Cuemath — hope the class just went well. I'm calling to check in. How did [Child's Name] find it? Because at the end of the day, [Child's Name] is the star of the show — their experience matters most."

[Stop. Wait. Let them speak fully.]

"And from your side — as a parent watching — what did you observe? Did the teacher's style feel like a good match for [Child's Name]?"

[Listen. Absorb. Do not interrupt.]

How to Read the Response

Positive / excited response → Move directly to Step 2. Close with confidence. Emotion is high — don't let it cool with too much more talking.

Neutral response ("it was okay", "fine") → Diagnose: was it Energy? Rapport? Talk Time? Ask: "What would have made it even better for [Child's Name]?" Then handle before closing.

Negative response ("my child didn't connect", "the teacher was explaining too much") → Do not close. Acknowledge. Offer a second trial. "I completely understand — not every teacher is the right match for every child. That's exactly why we have the trial. Can I arrange one more session with a different teacher? Same format, no cost."

2
Connect What They Saw to MathFit — 1–2 min
They've already been briefed. Now you're giving them the lens to interpret the experience.
AC Says

"[Parent], what you saw in that session today — that was Day 1 of the MathFit journey."

"Do you remember what I shared when we first spoke — about cue-based teaching? Today you saw it in action. The teacher wasn't explaining answers — they were asking questions, giving clues, guiding [Child's Name] to find their own answers. A teacher who explains builds dependency. A teacher who cues builds confidence."

"And that expectation you mentioned to me — [repeat the expectation they gave on the intro call] — that's exactly what this journey is designed to deliver. The first few months are foundation-building. That's what you saw today — the teacher diagnosing where [Child's Name] is. From here, the sessions get progressively more targeted to [Child's Name] specifically."

3
Make a Personalised Recommendation — 1–2 min
One recommendation. Specific to this child. Do not list options — make a decision for them.
AC Says

"Based on what the teacher observed today, and based on what you told me when we first spoke — my recommendation for [Child's Name] is the 12-month plan, 2 classes a week."

"Here's why specifically: [Child's Name] is [at Grade X, which is a critical foundation year / showing signs of [specific strength or gap from teacher notes]]. The 12-month plan gives enough time for the diagnostic work, the gap-filling, and then the actual MathFit compounding to show. You won't see the full shift in 3 months. Month 7–8 is where parents tell us things really click. You need to be in long enough for that to happen."

4
Walk Through What's Included — 2 min
Brief — they already know the broad picture. Just reinforce the specifics.
AC Says

"Here's what the 12-month plan includes for [Child's Name] — and I want to walk you through the curriculum specifically, because this is not what you'd expect."

"The Cuemath curriculum has four parts. Every single part is delivered the same way: 1:1, cue-based, with MathFit as the teaching philosophy. The teacher never explains — they guide. [Child's Name] does the thinking."

"Part 1: Grade-wise and board-wise academic curriculum. Fully aligned to [Child's Name]'s board — CBSE, ICSE, IB, Cambridge, whatever it is. Every concept, every chapter. But instead of the teacher going through it, [Child's Name] is guided to figure it out. That understanding sticks permanently."

"Part 2: Mental Maths curriculum. On the surface, this builds calculation speed. But the bigger benefit — and this is what parents tell us they notice after 3–4 months — is focus. When a child consistently practices mental computation, they're training their brain to hold attention and block out distraction. The same neural pathways that make you fast at mental math are the ones that stop you getting distracted during study. It transfers to every subject."

"Part 3: Enrichment — live real-world projects. Not word problems from a textbook. Real scenarios: what does a business need to break even? How does an architect calculate load? What does an investor actually do with percentages? [Child's Name] sees why math exists in the world — and that connection is what builds the Application pillar of MathFit. This is where the Interest and Relevance mindset shifts happen."

"Part 4: IMO preparation and HOTS — High Order Thinking Skills. Olympiad-style problems, pattern recognition, logical arguments, and problem types [Child's Name] has never seen before. This is the Reasoning pillar — and it's what separates strong school students from competitive exam performers. We build this from the ground up, from whatever grade [Child's Name] is in right now."

"On top of the curriculum: monthly PTMs — every month you sit with the teacher and hear exactly where [Child's Name] is on each pillar, what's been built, and what the next month focuses on. Full visibility. And the Smart Practice system tracks where [Child's Name] slows down and auto-generates targeted practice on that specific gap before moving forward."

5
Present the Investment + Risk Removal — 1 min
State the price. No hedging. Then immediately kill the risk.
AC Says

"The 12-month plan — 2 classes a week — is ₹98,176 including all GST. 104 classes."

"And [Parent] — the most important thing: we have a 100% pro-rata refund on all unused classes. If after any number of months you feel it's not delivering, you get back the full fee for every class [Child's Name] hasn't yet attended. No questions asked. 15 working days back to your original payment method. Zero risk. You only ever pay for what [Child's Name] actually attends."

[Stop. Do not fill the silence. Let the parent respond.]

6
Ask for the Decision — 30 sec
Ask directly. Get a clear answer. "Maybe" is not an answer.
AC Says

"So [Parent] — my recommendation for [Child's Name] is the 12-month plan. Shall we go ahead?"

[If YES: "Wonderful. I'm sending you the payment link right now on WhatsApp — it takes 5 minutes to complete. Once it's done, the teacher will be in touch to schedule the regular slot. Congratulations — [Child's Name]'s MathFit journey has officially started!"]

[If not yet: "Of course — what specifically would you like to think through? Let's address it now rather than have you decide without the full picture."]

Objections — Version A (Parent Already Knows MathFit)

These are objections that arise after a briefed parent has watched the trial. Handle them in order — address one fully before moving to the next.

Objection

"It's still expensive — can we start with 3 months?"

Counter

"I completely understand. And the 3-month plan is available — ₹30,680 for 26 classes. But let me be honest with you about why I didn't recommend it: the MathFit journey takes about 3 months just to get through the diagnostic phase and start building real momentum. If you do 3 months, you'll be evaluating at exactly the moment the compounding is beginning. Most parents who start on 3 months tell us the same thing — 'I wish I'd just gone for the year.'"

"If commitment is the concern, remember the refund policy — you can stop at any point and get back every unused class in full. That makes the 12-month plan effectively risk-free. You're not locked in — you're just securing the teacher's slot and the better price."

Objection

"The trial was good but I need to think / I'll call you back"

Counter

"Of course — what specifically is the part you'd like to think through? I'd rather we address it now while the experience is fresh. Is it the price, the commitment, or something about the trial?"

[Identify the real objection and handle it specifically. Then:]

"If you do want a day to reflect — I completely understand. But one thing I want you to know: each Cuemath teacher takes on a maximum of 35–40 students. That's a deliberate cap — it's what allows them to give each child the attention MathFit requires. [Teacher Name] has a limited number of slots left, and parents who've completed the trial — like [Child's Name] — get first right to choose their preferred slot. Once those slots fill, I'd either need to offer a different time or a different teacher entirely. When do you think you'll be ready to decide — tonight or tomorrow morning? I'll protect the slot as long as I can."

Objection

"My child said the trial was okay but didn't seem very excited"

Counter

"That's really useful feedback — and actually completely normal for a first session. Think about it: [Child's Name] just met a stranger, was asked to do math, and was being observed by a parent. Most children are a little guarded in the first session. The real energy comes from Session 3–4 onwards, once the teacher knows the child and has found their specific wavelength."

"What I'd ask is: was the teacher gentle and warm? Was [Child's Name] doing the thinking, or was the teacher mostly explaining? If the dynamic was right — even if the energy was quiet — that's a good sign. If you felt something was genuinely off, we can always schedule a second trial with a different teacher."

Objection

"My child is already doing well in school — I'm not sure this is necessary"

Counter

"That's actually our favourite type of student. We're not here to fix a problem — we're here to accelerate something already there. A child who is good at maths can become an exceptional thinker. School maths measures what [Child's Name] knows. MathFit builds how they think. The strongest students I've seen benefit the most — because they move from 'getting correct answers' to 'explaining why the answer is correct and what changes if the question changes.' That's the difference between a good student and an exceptional thinker."

Objection

"Let me discuss with my spouse — they weren't on the call"

Counter

"Of course. While you're discussing, I'll send a summary on WhatsApp — the plan I recommended, the pricing, and the refund policy — so you both have exactly the same information. When do you think you'll have had a chance to talk? Tonight? I can follow up first thing tomorrow morning."

"One thing to keep in mind for the conversation: the pro-rata refund means there's really no risk in starting. If it doesn't work — every unused class comes back. The only question is whether to start [Child's Name]'s MathFit journey now or delay it by another month."

Objection

"Can we start next month? We have holidays / exams / travel"

Counter

"Understood — and starting next month is fine. What I'd suggest: enrol now, and we'll schedule the first class for when you're back. That way the teacher's slot is secured — the same teacher [Child's Name] just met in the trial — and [Child's Name] doesn't lose another month on the waitlist.
